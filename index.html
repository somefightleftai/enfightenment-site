03 · The true opponent is yourself

52 · Cross the street

Where this comes from

Karate and yoga, started the same year at twenty-five. Thirty-seven years later both are still daily, and the other arts came in alongside them.

Fourteen years of daily training under Sensei Nishiyama, up to the end of his life. And under Aiko-san — his assistant, and a master in her own right. Sensei Nishiyama taught the class. Afterward Aiko-san taught hers, in the office.

She's the part nobody writes down. A ballet dancer who came to America around the same time Sensei Nishiyama did, and who taught him movement and breath. She'd tell us you have to take from Sensei Nishiyama — he doesn't always explain everything, so you watch, and you learn from the movement. She said those last years were his best teaching. Less hard repetition. More internal, more breath.

Twenty-seven years under Sensei Rokah, Sensei Nishiyama's top student — before, during, and after. He filled the gaps and drove the repetition the other two expected you to already have.

A decade of jiu-jitsu — Gracie Humaitá, then John Danaher, then B-Team. Now at Black Widow MMA: jiu-jitsu, Muay Thai, wrestling, and the karate I keep training on my own, all of it going into the same thing.

These teachings were handed down, tested, and paid for. None of them came out of a book.
